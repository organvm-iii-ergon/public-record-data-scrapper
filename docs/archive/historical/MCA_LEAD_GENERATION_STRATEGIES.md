## üîç PUBLIC RECORDS & GOVERNMENT DATA SOURCES

### 1. UCC Filings (The Foundation)
**What**: Uniform Commercial Code filings reveal existing merchant cash advances and loans
**Where**: Secretary of State websites in all 50 states
**Strategy**: 
- Scrape UCC-1 filings to identify businesses that have taken MCAs
- Target businesses 9-12 months after initial MCA (renewal window)
- Identify businesses with multiple stacked UCCs (desperate for capital, high risk)
- Track UCC-3 terminations (business just paid off MCA, may need new capital)

**State-Specific Resources:**
- California: https://bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov
- New York: https://www.dos.ny.gov/corps/bus_entity_search.html
- Texas: https://www.sos.state.tx.us/corp/sosda/
- Florida: https://dos.myflorida.com/sunbiz/
- Illinois: https://www.ilsos.gov/corporatellc/

**Advanced Techniques:**
- Cross-reference secured party names to identify competitor portfolios
- Analyze filing frequency to gauge business credit appetite
- Track amendment patterns to detect refinancing activity

### 2. Business Licenses & Permits
**What**: New business licenses indicate startups; permit applications show expansion
**Where**: 
- City/County Clerk offices
- State licensing boards (varies by industry)
- Federal: https://sam.gov for federal contractor registrations

**Strategy**:
- Target newly licensed businesses 6-12 months after opening (past initial capital, pre-profitability)
- Construction permits = expansion = capital needs
- Liquor licenses = high-revenue potential restaurants/bars
- Professional licenses (medical, legal) = stable, credit-worthy businesses

**High-Value Industries:**
- Restaurants: Food service permits, health department inspections
- Retail: Sales tax permits, occupancy permits
- Healthcare: Medical facility licenses, DEA registrations
- Construction: Contractor licenses, building permits

### 3. Property Records & Commercial Real Estate
**What**: Commercial lease filings, property purchases, build-outs
**Where**: 
- County Recorder offices
- CoStar (paid service)
- LoopNet for commercial listings
- Public GIS/property tax databases

**Strategy**:
- Businesses signing new commercial leases need capital for build-out
- Property purchases indicate expansion or relocation (capital intensive)
- Property tax increases suggest growing business value

**Data Points to Extract:**
- Lease square footage (larger = more capital needs)
- Tenant improvement allowances
- Multi-location operators (franchise potential)
- Recent renovations (building permits cross-reference)

### 4. Court Records & Legal Filings
**What**: Lawsuits, judgments, bankruptcies, liens
**Where**: 
- PACER (Federal courts): https://pacer.uscourts.gov
- State court systems (varies by state)
- County courthouse records

**Strategy**:
- **Judgment debtors**: Businesses with judgments may struggle to get bank loans (MCA opportunity)
- **Dismissed bankruptcies**: Recently dismissed Ch. 11 may need working capital
- **Lawsuit defendants**: Businesses facing lawsuits may need capital for legal fees/settlements
- **Avoid**: Active bankruptcies, fraudulent businesses, recent closures

**Red Flags to Filter:**
- Criminal charges against owners
- Repeated judgments (uncreditworthy)
- Recent tax liens (IRS issues)

### 5. Tax & Financial Filings
**What**: Business tax registrations, sales tax permits, payroll tax filings
**Where**:
- State Department of Revenue websites
- IRS EIN lookup (limited public access)
- State sales tax databases

**Strategy**:
- Sales tax permit applications = new retail/restaurant
- Payroll tax filings = hiring = growth
- Tax exemption certificates = specific industries (non-profits usually excluded)

### 6. Federal Contract Awards
**What**: Government contract winners announced publicly
**Where**: 
- https://sam.gov (System for Award Management)
- https://www.fpds.gov (Federal Procurement Data System)
- https://www.usaspending.gov

**Strategy**:
- Businesses winning federal contracts need working capital for fulfillment
- Track small business set-asides (under $10M contracts)
- Time outreach 30-60 days after award announcement
- Best industries: IT services, construction, professional services

**Data to Extract:**
- Contract value (size capital needs)
- Award date (timing for outreach)
- Performance period (ongoing capital needs)
- NAICS codes (industry verification)

### 7. Professional Licensing Boards

## ‚öñÔ∏è LEGAL & ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Data Privacy & Compliance
- **GDPR**: If targeting EU businesses, ensure consent
- **CCPA**: California privacy rights (minimize PII collection)
- **TCPA**: Telephone Consumer Protection Act (cold calling rules)
- **CAN-SPAM**: Email marketing compliance
- **Fair Lending Laws**: No discrimination based on protected classes

### Ethical Scraping
- **Respect robots.txt**: Don't scrape if explicitly forbidden
- **Rate Limiting**: Avoid overwhelming servers (DDoS-like behavior)
- **Attribution**: If republishing data, credit sources
- **Accuracy**: Verify data before contacting businesses (wrong numbers = spam)

### Public Records Access
- **Legal**: Public records are public, scraping is generally legal
- **Terms of Service**: Some portals prohibit automated access (check ToS)
- **Commercial Use**: Some states restrict commercial use of public data
- **Bulk Downloads**: Some states offer bulk data purchases (cheaper, legal)
